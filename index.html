üìã Standard Project Structure

1. Title Page

Project title, your name, institution, department, supervisor, and submission date

2. Abstract (200-300 words)

Brief summary of problem, methodology, key findings, and conclusions

3. Table of Contents

List all chapters, sections, figures, and tables with page numbers

4. Introduction

Background, problem statement, objectives, scope, and significance of study

5. Literature Review

Critical analysis of existing research, theoretical framework, and knowledge gaps

6. Methodology

Study area, data collection methods, analytical techniques, equipment, and procedures

7. Results and Discussion

Present findings with tables, graphs, and charts; interpret and discuss implications

8. Conclusions and Recommendations

Summary of key findings, practical recommendations, and future research directions

9. References

All cited sources in standard format (APA, Harvard, or IEEE)

10. Appendices

Raw data, calculations, additional figures, questionnaires, or supplementary materials
